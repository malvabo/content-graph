## 8 Insights Found

### 🔬 Science Claims (3)

1. **"60,000 times faster than text"** — No peer-reviewed source. Originated from a 3M corporate presentation.
   → Rephrase: *"far faster than text — some researchers estimate tens of thousands of times quicker"*

2. **"remember 10% of what they hear"** — Edgar Dale's Cone of Experience percentages are widely considered a myth in learning science.
   → Rephrase: *"people retain far more when visuals reinforce what they hear"*

3. **"the first 30 seconds matter most"** — The specific figure isn't well-sourced. Attention span research varies widely.
   → Rephrase: *"your opening moments set the tone for everything that follows"*

### 🎭 Metaphors (4)

4. **"rewiring the neural pathways"** → *"reshaping how our audience thinks and feels"*
5. **"the butterfly effect"** → *"Even a tiny shift in your opening line can send the entire talk down a different emotional path"*
6. **"they're memory anchors"** → *"they're the hooks that make your ideas stick"*
7. **"a mirror of how the human psyche processes transformation"** → *"it maps directly onto how people naturally experience change"*

### ⚠️ Logic Gaps (1)

8. **"Every great talk follows this ancient pattern without exception"** — Absolute claim easy to disprove. Many successful talks don't follow the hero's journey.
   → Rephrase: *"Many of the most memorable talks echo this ancient pattern"*
